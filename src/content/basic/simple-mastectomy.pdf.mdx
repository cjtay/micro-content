The surgeon removes the entire breast, including the nipple, areola, fascia (covering) of the pectoralis major muscle (primary chest muscle), and skin.

Depending on the situation, a few underarm lymph nodes might be removed as part of a sentinel lymph node biopsy. If hospitalised, most women can go home the next day.
